Dr.Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan

About the Legend

Who Was Dr. Radhakrishnan?

Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan was a famous teacher, philosopher, and leader who became the President of India. He believed education was the key to progress and was known for his deep knowledge of Indian culture and philosophy.

Early Life

Born: September 5, 1888, in Tamil Nadu, India. He came from a simple family and studied hard to become a brilliant student.

Teaching and Philosophy

Dr. Radhakrishnan became a teacher of philosophy. He explained Indian philosophy to the world and believed in the idea of unity between all religions. He wrote many important books about religion and Indian philosophy.

President of India

Dr. Radhakrishnan served as the Vice-President (1952-1962) and the President of India (1962-1967). As a leader, he focused on peace and education.

Teachers' Day

To honor his love for teaching, his birthday on September 5 is celebrated as Teachers' Day in India. He believed teachers play a key role in building a strong nation.

Famous Words

"Teachers should be the best minds in the country."

"Education gives us wings to fly."

Honors

He was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest award, in 1954 for his contributions to education and philosophy.
